Issues in Wilderness Recreation

Inspiration

Outside of any professional ambitions, most of my passions are related to enjoying the natural beauty of the world. Some of the best days of my life have been spent away from any connection to technology, enjoying the natural beauty and solitude of the wilderness. Whether its hiking, backcountry skiing, or climbing I believe everyone can benefit from some peace, quiet, and connection to the outdoor world. Because of this, I have decided to address the question of how we can improve access to outdoor recreation while reducing our impact on the planet.

The Problem

Unfortunately planning a trip into the wilderness is not an easy task, and requires consolidating information across a variety of sources. Additionally, to keep wilderness areas pristine, permits are often required for camping or day use, and can be confusing to obtain. Due to their annoying nature, permits are often not filled out, which leaves the agencies who maintain public land with incomplete information about the types of use their areas are seeing. A perfect example is the Olympic National Park backcountry permits site. It has disjointed information, and does not offer a way to obtain permits online. Furthermore, there are opportunities to make natural recreation more accessible for those who are disabled or disadvantaged, and these are the people who would benefit the most from a closer connection to the natural world.

Potential Web App Solutions

Bellow I have listed a couple of ideas that could solve information problems that are present in the world of outdoor recreation.

  • Wilderness Planning and Reservation System
    • Create a better interface for reserving wilderness permits
    • Join with weather and route information to make planning simple
  • Improving access for Individuals with Disabilities
    • Create a comprehensive list of areas where it is possible for individuals with disabilities to enjoy the outdoors
    • Build new tools to make planning for disabled persons simple
